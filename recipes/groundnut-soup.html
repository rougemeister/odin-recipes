Groundnut Soup

A Hot Bowl of Beef Groundnut soup

Groundnut Soup is a soup made from peanuts, often with various other ingredients.

It is a staple of African cuisine but is also eaten in East Asia, the United States and other areas around the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 medium yellow onions (about 12 ounces; 340g), halved and ends trimmed, divided
  • 2 cups (480ml) homemade or store-bought low-sodium chicken broth, plus more as needed, divided
  • 5 medium cloves garlic, divided
  • 1 ounce (28g) fresh ginger (about a 1-inch knob), divided
  • 2 teaspoons tomato paste
  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken legs (about 2 pounds; 900g)
  • 1 hot chili pepper, such as bird's eye, habanero, or Scotch bonnet (see note)
  • bay leaves
  • 1 cup creamy peanut butter (9 ounces; 255g)
  • 1 (28-ounce; 794g) can plum tomatoes
  • 1 whole smoke-dried fish, such as tilapia or snapper (see note)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Hot cooked white rice or fufu, to serve (see notes)

Steps

  1. In a blender, purée 2 onion halves, 1/2 cup (120ml) chicken stock, 3 garlic cloves, 1/2 ounce ginger, and tomato paste.
  2. In a Dutch oven, combine chicken legs with purée, remaining onion halves, remaining 1/2 ounce ginger, and remaining 2 cloves garlic, along with hot pepper and bay leaves.
  3. Toss to coat.
  4. Set Dutch oven over medium heat and bring to a simmer, then cover, reduce heat to low, and cook until halved onion is soft and translucent, about 20 minutes.
  5. Add an extra 1/2 cup (120ml) chicken stock if mixture begins to stick to the bottom of the pot.
  6. Transfer chunks of onion, ginger, garlic cloves, and hot pepper to blender.
  7. Add peanut butter, canned tomatoes and their juices, and remaining 1 1/2 cups (360ml) chicken stock and purée until smooth.
  8. Pass blended mix through a fine-mesh strainer into the Dutch oven, stirring to incorporate.
  9. Increase heat to medium and bring to a simmer.
  10. Lower heat to medium-low and cook.
  11. Stir occasionally, until chicken is tender, oils have surfaced, and mixture has thickened and reduced by about one-third.
  12. Add smoked fish, reduce heat to low, cover pot, and cook an additional 5 minutes.
